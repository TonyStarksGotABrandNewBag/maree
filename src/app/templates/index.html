M.A.R.E.E. is built for organizations the security industry leaves behind — the school district, the rural clinic, the small-business IT person doing their own malware screening. She tells you what she sees, why she's blocking it, and how confident she is. She blocks by default when uncertain.

What the three verdicts mean

ALLOWED High-confidence benign. The file is released to the user.
BLOCKED — Malware High-confidence malicious. The file is quarantined; M.A.R.E.E. explains why and what to do next.
BLOCKED — Uncertain Low confidence in either direction. The file is blocked pending human review (zero-trust failure mode).

The ensemble must affirmatively allow. Silence, disagreement, or low confidence all yield BLOCK.
